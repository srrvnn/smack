Registration mandatory for Afghan nationals'

By Our Special Correspondent

NEW DELHI, SEPT. 18. In a move to tighten the security apparatus in the aftermath of terrorist strikes in New York and Washington and the plans of the U.S. to strike in Afghanistan for hunting down Osama bin Laden, the Government today revised its policy on the stay of Afghan nationals in India, making their registration mandatory with the authorities.

The Government also brought the Bureau of Immigration, which was till now functioning under the Intelligence Bureau, directly under the Foreigners Division of the Union Home Ministry with immediate effect. All Afghan nationals, possessing valid or expired documents, would now be registered.

The cases of those who are not in possession of any documents would be considered by a committee set up in the Home Ministry, according to a Ministry spokesman here.
