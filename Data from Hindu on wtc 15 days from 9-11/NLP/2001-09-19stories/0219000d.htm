Shahi Imam threatens protest

By Our Staff Reporter

NEW DELHI, SEPT. 18. India would not allow the U.S. to use its airspace or soil to mount a war on Afghanistan to ``smoke out'' Osama bin Laden, the Shahi Imam of Jama Masjid, Syed Ahmed Bukhari, today said quoting an assurance given to him by the Prime Minister, Mr. Atal Behari Vajpayee, over telephone.

Addressing a press conference here, after the telephonic conversation with Mr. Vajpayee on Monday, the Shahi Imam said: ``The Prime Minister promised me that his Government will not provide any logistical support to the U.S. army like airbase or fueling of its planes for attacking Afghanistan. Mr. Vajpayee said India would only provide certain intelligence inputs on terrorism to the U.S.''

The Shahi Imam said he had telephoned Mr. Vajpayee to convey the community's resentment over media reports that India will be providing logistical support to the U.S. army. ``When I told Mr. Vajpayee that I will be stating this (assurance) at a press conference and pass on his promise to the public, he said I can do that,'' the Shahi Imam claimed.

Referring to his conversation with Mr. Vajpayee, he said: ``I specifically informed him there will be a nation-wide protest against the Government if it offered any help to the U.S. in killing the innocent people of Afghanistan.'' At the same time, he said that the protests will be peaceful. ``We are peace loving people and we want peace in our country.''

Condemning Pakistan's decision to provide support to the U.S., the Shahi Imam said: ``If the U.S. attacks Afghanistan from the soil of Pakistan, the reaction of about 50 lakh Afghan refugees living in the country would be so severe that it cannot be controlled and might very well lead to the disintegration of Pakistan.''

Without elaborating, the Shahi Imam said he was in touch with Muslim leaders of other countries on this issue. ``I have already spoken to some and will be holding consultation with others in the next few days. But, these do not include Pakistan and Afghanistan,'' he said.

The U.S. Ambassador, Mr. Robert D. Blackwill, will meet the Shahi Imam at the Jama Masjid here on Thursday to discuss on the crisis in the aftermath of the terrorists attack in the U.S.

Earlier, mounting a scathing attack on the U.S., the Imam said: ``The proposed attack on Afghanistan is an action of revenge. It can never be called a war against terrorism. Any attack on Afghanistan will be considered as an attack on the entire Muslim world.''

On the U.S. allegation that Osama bin Laden was responsible for the attack on it, the Imam said: ``The U.S. has not provided any valid documentary proof of it. Even if we tend to believe what the U.S. claims then it is no more a superpower. Osama bin Laden is the new superpower.''

The Imam demanded that the U.S. should desist from any attack on innocent people of Afghanistan. ``If there is any proof against Osama, he should be tried in the International Court of Justice as per international law. The U.S. should keep in mind that the arrest of Osama or the destruction of Afghanistan is not a solution to the issue. The only solution lies in the revision of its policies towards Palestine and Iraq.''
