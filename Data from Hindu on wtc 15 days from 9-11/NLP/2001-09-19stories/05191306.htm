A place for the neutral

Sir, - The U.S. Secretary of State, Gen. Colin Powell, makes an interesting statement: ``...as we reach out to countries asking for their help, (we tell them) you are either with us or against us''. There is no scope for the neutral-minded. Within the Islamic groups, some are against terrorism per se but are also against some of the foreign policies followed by the U.S. They might choose to stay neutral vis-a-vis the U.S. resolution to attack Afghanistan in the `hope' of eliminating the suspect, Osama Bin Laden.

Even during World War II, some countries stayed neutral. Any democratic concept must accommodate the neutral-minded. Otherwise it becomes unilateral.

There is a good possibility of the Afghan mercenaries crossing over into Jammu and Kashmir simply because they are used to it. Pakistan may allow select groups to do so while taking time to partially agree or disagree to the charter of demands given by the U.S. India will have to keep this in mind while agreeing to the demands that are made on it.

Approximately 50,000 families in Jammu and Kashmir lost their relatives in a decade of cross-border terrorism, euphemistically called the `independent struggle'. They suffered silent mourning. Nobody organised any `Day of remembrance' for them. India could not say to other nations, ``Either you are with us or against us''. Repeated assertion of terrorism as the cause failed to arouse world opinion though 17 per cent of the world's population live in India.

Sqn. Ldr. B. G. Prakash,

Bangalore
