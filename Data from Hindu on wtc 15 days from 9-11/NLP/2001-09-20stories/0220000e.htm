India waiting for U.S. decision

By Vinay Kumar

NEW DELHI, SEPT. 19. India is watching what kind of decision the U.S. would take on the retaliatory attacks it plans against Afghanistan and Osama bin Laden, who is allegedly behind the strikes on Washington and New York. Government sources today indicated that New Delhi was not taking anything for granted.

The Government has noted with satisfaction, the categorical assurance given by the American Ambassador, Mr. Robert Blackwill, to the Union Home Minister, Mr. L.K. Advani, that the U.S. would not view the problem of terrorism in a ``piece-meal'' manner. The American envoy, who met Mr. Advani twice after the September 11 attacks, is learnt to have told Mr. Advani that there had been a ``radical change'' in the approach of the U.S. administration towards terrorism. Mr. Blackwill reportedly said that Pakistan President, Gen. Pervez Musharraf, should understand if action against Taliban and Osama bin Laden was the first phase there would be a second phase as well.

PTI, UNI report:

Mr. Blackwill assured India that Washington would not make Kashmir a negotiating point while talking to Pakistan, highly- placed official sources said here.

This is believed to have been conveyed in an interaction with Mr. L.K. Advani, the Parliamentary Affairs Minister, Mr. Pramod Mahajan, and the Chief of Naval Staff, Admiral Sushil Kumar.

Meeting cancelled

Mr. Blackwill has cancelled his meeting with the Shahi Imam of Jama Masjid, Syed Ahmad Bukhari, scheduled for tomorrow in the wake of the Imam's virulent attack on the U.S. On Tuesday, he had called upon the entire Islamic world to unitedly fight any retaliatory attack on Afghanistan.
