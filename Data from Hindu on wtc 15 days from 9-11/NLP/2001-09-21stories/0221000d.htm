Hurriyat opposes strike call

By Shujaat Bukhari

SRINAGAR, SEPT. 20. Ostensibly distancing itself from the ``pan- Islamic movements worldwide'', the All-Party Hurriyat Conference (APHC), in a clear rebuff to militants, has opposed the strike call given by them and extended full support to the Pakistan President, Gen. Pervez Musharraf's stand vis-a-vis the U.S. It described the issue of Osama bin Laden as irrelevant to Kashmiris and said developments in Afghanistan would have no impact here.

After chairing an extraordinary meeting of the executive, the Hurriyat chief, Prof. Abdul Ghani Bhat, told presspersons that Gen. Musharraf's stand was ``realistic, forthright and bold'' and in the right direction. He hoped the issues in Afghanistan would be resolved through negotiations.

Opposing the strike call given by the Pak-Afghan Defence Council and supported by militant groups here, he said, ``We appeal to the people of Kashmir not to give heed to any call given by any body other than the Hurriyat Conference,'' and added that people should carry on with their normal business.

The Hurriyat is ready to face any situation and has decided to instill discipline at all levels. There should be no room for discussions about the APHC being in trouble or confused on issues like elections, he said.

Distancing the APHC from Islamic movements worldwide, Prof. Bhat said Kashmir was a political struggle and an indigenous one. On Osama bin Laden, who has been declared the ``greatest warrior of Islam'' by Kashmiri militants, he said, ``the Hurriyat won't get involved in issues which are irrelevant to us''.

On fighting terrorism at the global level, he said: ``we are not opposed to it and have made it clear in a letter to the U.S. President, Mr. George W. Bush, while extending sympathies to the U.S. people''.

But there is a dire need to differentiate between terrorism from the fight for freedom, he said, adding that ``India has to shun rigidity and address the Kashmir issue as core one involving the future of millions of people''.

The meeting was attended by Mr. Abdul Gani Lone, Mirwaiz Umar Farooq, Syed Ali Geelani, Moulvi Abbas Ansari, Mr. Khalil Mohammad Khalil and Mr. Bashir Ahmed Bhat. However, Mr. Geelani, a known hardliner, stayed away from the press meet.
