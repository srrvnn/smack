International manhunt to nab Omar Sheikh

LONDON, SEPT. 23. An international manhunt has been launched to nab Ahmed Omar Sheikh, an Islamic militant who was freed from a Jammu jail in exchange for the release of 154 passengers of a hijacked Indian Airlines flight in 1999, for his suspected links with Osama bin Laden.

British security services want him for questioning to probe his link with Osama, the prime suspect in the recent terrorist attacks in the U.S., a report in The Sunday Times said.

One of the hijackers of the IA flight is suspected to be involved in the terrorist attacks in the U.S. and the police here think that it could be Omar Sheikh, who studied in a London prep school and graduated from the London School of Economics. How the boy from a well-to-do family in Wanstead, east London, came to join the world of Islamic terrorism provided an insight into the kind of people such organisations were looking to recruit, it said.

Omar Sheikh first emerged as a key figure in the Islamic movement in 1994, when he kidnapped three Britons and an American in Jammu and Kashmir. He was caught when police stormed his hideout. However, after three years in prison awaiting trial, all charges against him were dropped after he and two other Islamic militants were freed in exchange for the release of the 154 hostages of the IA flight. Since his release, Omar Sheikh has disappeared.

- PTI
