Rocca to meet Taliban leader

By B. Muralidhar Reddy

ISLAMABAD, JULY 29. The American Assistant Secretary of State, Ms. Christina Rocca, is expected to meet representatives of the ruling Taliban in Afghanistan during her visit to Pakistan beginning tomorrow.

A Pakistani news agency, NNI, has reported that the meeting, to be held here, is being arranged at the instance of U.S diplomats. Ms. Rocca is likely to meet the Afghan Ambassador, Mulla Abdul Salam Zaeef and convey Washington's concern over Osama bin Laden's presence in Afghanistan.

It will be the second diplomatic contact between a U.S official and Taliban representative in less than two months. The former U.S. Ambassador to Pakistan, Mr. William B Milam, met his Afghan counterpart, Mulla Zaeef, and told him that Taliban would be responsible if Osama's supporters attacked any American national.

The United States and Taliban have held a series of talks but have so far failed to resolve any of their dispute, especially the Osama problem. The grievance of Taliban is that Washington has not bothered to respond to three specific proposals they have floated to find a solution to the issue. The United States, however, says that the proposals do not address the demands of the U.N Security Council resolutions about terrorism and expulsion of Osama.

The U.S official is likely to take up America's deep concerns over what it calls terrorist threats to the U.S citizens and Taliban's shelter to Osama.
