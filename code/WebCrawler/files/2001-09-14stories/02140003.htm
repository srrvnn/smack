No flights yet to U.S.

By Gargi Parsai

NEW DELHI, SEPT. 13. International flights from India to Europe resumed today and, although air space in the U.S. and Canada were opened late tonight, no flights operated out of India to the U.S. till midnight. The priority of international airlines would now be to complete flights that were either diverted or recalled or forced to land at the nearest airport in the middle of their journey on Tuesday when the terrorist attacks were made on the World Trade Center and the Pentagon in the U.S.

Air-India has cancelled its daily flight to New York via London, but will fly its Friday terminator flight to London tomorrow. Earlier, the British authorities had banned international flights overflying London city. However, today a British Airways spokesperson said all services out of India to London were operational. Today BA resumed its service out of the U.K. to Tel Aviv but its flights to Pakistan, the U.S. and Canada remained cancelled.

The airspace in the U.S. remained closed till the Federal Aviation Administration were convinced that more stringent security procedures were in place at all airports across the country to ensure safety and security of passengers.

Unlike in India where physical frisking and physical checking of hand baggage were introduced after the hijacking of Delhi-bound IC-814 from Kathmandu to Kandahar, airport security has been unobtrusive in the U.S. Domestic travel, in particular, in the fiercely competitive U.S. has been easy and hassle-free. Security measures have been more dependent on sophisticated equipment. But the hijacking of the four United Airlines and American Airlines flights operating in domestic sector for terrorist operations on Tuesday has changed all that.

Passengers on domestic flights in the U.S. will now have to go through stringent security checks. Only ticketed passengers would be allowed beyond security points. Passengers holding electronic tickets shall be required to show paper receipts for proof of ticket purchase. All curbside and off-airport check-in would be discontinued. And, passengers would now have to report two hours before departure for domestic flights and three hours before international flights.

In view of the large scale disruption of operations all over the world, airlines have relaxed their ticket refund and journey postponement norms. Passengers with ticketed travel between September 11 and 15 will be allowed to change travel dates for new travel during September (different with different airlines) with no penalty or change fee. Both United Airlines and American Airlines (not operating in India) have allowed refund of ticketed travel from for two weeks from September 11 to the original form of payment or by voucher.
