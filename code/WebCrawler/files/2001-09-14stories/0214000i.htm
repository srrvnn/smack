Indo-U.S. Finance Ministers' meet postponed

By Our Special Correspondent

NEW DELHI, SEPT. 13. The Indo-U.S. sub-Cabinet level meeting of the Finance Ministers proposed to be held here next month has been postponed in view of the terrorist attacks in New York and Washington. This was disclosed today by the Finance Minister, Mr. Yashwant Sinha, after a meeting with the U.S. Ambassador- designate, Mr. Robert Blackwill. He said the incidents would not have any immediate direct impact on the Indian economy or on the value of the rupee.

Mr. Yashwant Sinha said the Indo-U.S. Finance Ministers meeting will now be held around the time of the G-20 Finance Ministers meeting being held here in mid-November. Mr. Sinha described the assaults on the World Trade Center in New York and the Pentagon in Washington as ``unfortunate''.
