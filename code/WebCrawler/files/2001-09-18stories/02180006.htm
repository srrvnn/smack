Dhindsa expresses concern over attacks on Sikhs

By Our Special Correspondent

NEW DELHI, SEPT. 17. The Union Minister for Chemicals and Fertilizers and senior Akali Dal leader, Mr. Sukhdev Singh Dhindsa, expressed serious concern over the attacks on Sikhs due to ``mistaken identity.''

The Counsellor (Political) in the U.S. Embassy here, Dr. Robert Bogg, who called on Mr. Dhindsa said the U.S. administration was ``very concerned'' and dubbed the attacks as serious incidents of crime.

Mr. Dhindsa said he suggested to the U.S. official to educate Americans about the identity of Sikhs. The Vice-Chairman of the National Commission for Minorities, Mr. Tarlochan Singh, was present during the meeting.

It is learnt that Mr. Dhindsa is planning to visit the U.S. soon. Political leaders and prominent academicians from the Sikh community held a meeting at Mr. Dhindsa's residence.
