United Airlines pulls out of India

By Our Special Correspondent

NEW DELHI, SEPT. 17. The first casualty of the 20 per cent cut in United Airlines' capacity, is India. The airlines today announced withdrawal of its service out of India and blamed it on the terrorist attacks in the U.S. The non-stop service was reintroduced this April.

``As United Airlines cuts capacity and services in order to respond to the new business and security environments that follow last week's attacks, the India service is being discontinued,'' the airline said here today - its first public statement in New Delhi since the September 11 attacks. Two of the airline's hijacked passenger carriers were involved in the attacks forcing the airline to cut capacity by 20 per cent. The airline, which has been on the ground since then will have its U.S.-bound passengers accommodated on other airlines.

After a week of disruption in services due to closure of American and Canadian airspace, most airlines tottered back to normality. Air India resumed its Mumbai-Delhi-London-New York service from today. It will operate flights to Chicago from tomorrow.

British Airways, Lufthansa, Singapore Airlines, Virgin Atlantic have also resumed near-normal services. British Airways is not still operating to Islamabad and Virgin Atlantic is not flying on the London-Boston sector as the Boston airspace is still closed. Cathay Pacific also announced that it will operate all of its six return passengers services between Hong Kong and North America. It said that in-town check-in service at Hong Kong and Kowloon Stations was not available for the North America flights.

In its renewed security guidelines, Air India has advised passengers to restrict the weight of the one hand baggage to five kg and report for check-in for U.S.-bound flights at least 4 hours before scheduled departure. Check-in counters would close 90 minutes before departure. No metal knives/pocket knives or scissors of any size would be permitted in the baggage.
