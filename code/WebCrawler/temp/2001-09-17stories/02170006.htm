Pak.'s 'sensitivities'will be noted: Powell

NEW DELHI, SEPT. 16. The U.S. will take into account Pakistan's ``sensitivities'' against involvement of India and Israel in any operation to deal with those responsible for the recent terrorist attacks in New York and Washington, the Secretary of State, Mr. Colin Powell, said tonight.

Responding to a question about Pakistan's stand that they would cooperate with Washington in dealing with Osama bin Laden - suspected to be behind the terrorist attacks - and the Taliban regime which is hosting him, Mr. Powell said: ``We understand their sensitivities that would be involved in anything that might involve India and Israel. We will take those sensitivities into account.''

However, he told CNN in an interview, ``at the end of the day we will do what is appropriate and necessary''. The interviewer told Mr. Powell that Pakistan does not want India and Israel to be involved presumably because it would embarrass Islamabad.

Mr. Powell said, ``well, we will see what their (Pakistan) position is. We will talk to them. Right now, we do not have a multi-national force going anywhere.'' He said that the U.S. is dispatching ``in the next several days'' a team to Islamabad to discuss specific American requirements in relation to any operation that might be undertaken in the region.

Mr. Powell refused to go into the details of whether Pakistan had agreed to allow use of its airspace or military bases on the plea that the two countries were involved in ``sensitive diplomatic discussions at present.''

Mr. Powell also gave an ultimatum to the Taliban to choose whether it wanted to be ``at the receiving end of the full wrath of the U.S. and others or to get rid of Osama bin Laden and his organisation (Al Qida).''

``You either respond, rip them up, help us rip them up or you will suffer the consequences,'' he told the Kabul regime.

Mr. Powell said the campaign against terrorism would be long- term. In the near term, the U.S. would go after specific organisations behind the terror attacks in the United States.

He said that citizens of 40 countries had lost their lives in the attacks on the World Trade Center. The U.S. was not talking to the Taliban right now but expected to do so in the days ahead.

Mr. Powell said he wanted to make it clear to the Taliban that they must comply with previous directions from the U.N. and other organisations ``to stop this, to expel the Al Qida, to destroy it and help us destroy it.''

Asked if he expected the Taliban to cooperate with the U.S. to get Osama bin Laden, Mr. Powell said: ``I am not carrying any expectation. I am only looking for results. They either do or don't. It's binary: yes or no.''
