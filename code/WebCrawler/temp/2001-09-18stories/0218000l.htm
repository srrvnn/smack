Joke on Osama lands pilots in trouble

By Prashant Pandey

NEW DELHI, SEPT. 17. Dropping the name of Osama bin Laden, even in a lighter vein, after the September 11 devastation in the U.S. may not be a safe proposition as three young pilot friends found out after high drama extending over two days.

On Saturday afternoon, the Police Control Room here received a frantic call from a resident of Anand Parbat in west Delhi. The caller said he had received a message on his mobile phone suggesting that he join the Osama camp as pilot as he was offering a fabulous sum.

Police immediately swung into action and on the basis of the sender's number discovered that the call was actually meant for Capt. Jitendra Dogra, a private airline pilot living here. Capt. Dogra was brought to the police station and grilled by officials of the Delhi police, the Intelligence Bureau and other intelligence agencies for well over nine hours.

As it turned out, Mr. Anees Singh, a pilot with another airline and living in Hyderabad, had intended to send the ``sensational'' message in jest to his friend, Capt. Dogra, in Delhi. However, he dialled the wrong number and the message landed on the cell phone of the Anand Parbat resident.

However, this was not the end of the drama as the security agencies were not prepared to leave anything to chance. In support of his claim that the message was in jest, Capt. Dogra had to reveal messages of such nature which he had received from other pilot friends. ``The messages were mostly about their private lives and general fun that goes on between friends,'' a police officer said.

During interrogation, the name of a third friend, also a pilot with a private airline and based in Mumbai, cropped up. The agencies informed their counterparts in Hyderabad and Mumbai about the ``findings'', following which the two friends were questioned at length and the entire ``story'' given by Capt. Dogra corroborated.

It was only after the agencies were fully convinced that the three pilots actually did not intend any harm that they were let off. The entire exercise was called off today after all doubts were cleared. For the pilots, however, Osama bin Laden has become a ``dreaded name''.
