GoM soon for rural, urban housing

By Our Special Correspondent

NEW DELHI, SEPT. 17. In a bid to boost up housing activity, the Prime Minister, Mr. Atal Behari Vajpayee, has decided to constitute a Group of Ministers (GoM) to address all issues concerning rural and urban housing.

A formal decision was made following a comprehensive review of initiatives taken by the Government in relation to the rural and urban housing sector today. The Union Finance Minister, the Deputy Chairman, Planning Commission, the Rural Development Minister and the Union Minister for Urban Development and Poverty Alleviation and the Union Law Minister have been made members of the GoM.

The meeting, chaired by Mr. Vajpayee, also decided to convene a National Housing Conference in the near future. The State Chief Ministers, Finance Ministers and Housings Ministers would be involved in the conference in order to ensure effective implementation of the decisions and initiatives finalised during the deliberations.

The agenda for the conference will among other things include identification of specific action areas to facilitate a strategy for meeting the growing housing needs of the country. Follow-up action that needs to be taken by States in view of the repeal of the Urban Land Ceiling Acts and amendments and notifications under the Rent Control Acts would also be considered.

The conference will also consider initiatives for new housing projects both in rural and urban areas besides reforms that need to be introduced to make housing boards and urban local bodies more effective.

Earlier, the Ministries of Rural and Urban Development made detailed presentations with the focus on housing initiatives. Other issues that figured prominently at the meeting included amendment to the State Urban Land Ceiling Act as well as the Rent Control Act, need for reduction in stamp duties by various State Governments, introduction of insurance component in housing for poor, increasing unit cost of rural housing from Rs. 20,000 to Rs. 30,000.

Launching of Valmiki-Ambedkar Basti Awas Yojana and doubling of equity of HUDCO from Rs. 1,250 crores to Rs. 2,500 crores for expanding support to housing activity was also considered.

Mr. Vajpayee underlined the need for increasing the thrust on housing, as it would kick start the economy besides providing significant forwards and backward linkages with crucial sectors of economy and stimulating mass employment in the construction sector.

It was observed that under the two million housing programme scheme launched in 1998-99, over 25 lakh rural houses were taken up as against the target of 13 lakh houses each year. Similarly, 8.5 lakh housing units were taken up as part of the urban housing as against the goal of 7 lakh units.

The review meeting was attended by Ministers in the two concerned Ministries besides the Finance Minister, Mr. Yashwant Sinha and the Deputy Chairman of the Planning Commission, Mr. K.C. Pant.
