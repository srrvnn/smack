Inconsistent stand

Sir, - The terrorist attacks in the U.S. have pushed the Indian leaders into utter confusion. During the all-party meeting held in Delhi last week the Government had received cooperation for its stand taken in tackling the situation in the aftermath of the attacks. But once the Opposition leaders came out of the meeting they sang a different tune. The former Prime Ministers, Mr. V. P. Singh and Mr. Deve Gowda, criticised the Government for backing the U.S. on the issue. The Leftists too have expressed unhappiness. They find no national interest in joining the U.S. to combat terrorism.The FBI has arrested three suspected terrorists with Indian passports. The epicenter of terrorist activities may be Pakistan and its kingpin, Osama bin Laden, may be in Afghanistan, but his agents are spread all over the world, including India.

V. A. Gopala,

Bangalore

Sir, - Our Prime Minister has categorically stated that, ``Every Indian has to be a part of this global war on terrorism... we must and will stamp out this evil from our land and from the world''. On moral grounds and in our own national interests, we should stand united and support the stand taken by the Prime Minister. When a handful of unidentified terrorists are capable of attacking the most powerful nation in the world, we would be stranded without support when such a thing happens to our country

All the parties endorsed the stand taken by Mr. Vajpayee at the all-party meeting for a consensus on global terrorism. But later Mr. V.P. Singh and the Communists expressed reservations over the support to the U.S. In America, the people, the Opposition, the Congress and the Senate have given unstinted support to the President's plan of action.

S. Venugopalan,

Chennai
