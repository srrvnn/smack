Advani discounts Pak. claim on support to U.S.

By Our Special Correspondent

AHMEDABAD, SEPT. 18. The Union Home Minister, Mr. L.K. Advani, has said that Pakistan could not have put any conditions to the United States for support in the possible military action against Afghanistan. Addressing an election meeting here on Monday night, Mr. Advani said Pakistan did not have the courage and the capacity to place any condition before the U.S. It was only to ``satisfy'' its domestic constituency that Pakistan made such ``misleading'' claims of conditional support in the fight against terrorism.

He said the U.S. President, Mr. George W. Bush, had in a telephonic conversation with the Prime Minister, Mr. Atal Behari Vajpayee, made it clear that Pakistan had not suggested any conditions nor would any condition, such as intervention in Kashmir, be acceptable to the U.S.

He said a war was ``inevitable' but the time and arena would be decided by the U.S. The war against terrorism was not the problem of the U.S. alone and all democratic countries should be prepared for it. Mr. Advani said India had been fighting terrorism for the last 50 years but it was for the first time that other countries realised the menace of terrorist activities.

Stating that Pakistan was ``paying for its misdeeds in the past,'' he said the Pakistan President, Gen. Pervez Musharraf, was facing a Catch-22 situation at home.

If Pakistan did not join the war against terrorism, it would have to face the wrath of the superpowers, and if it did, Taliban would not allow it to live in peace.

Pak. buying time: Abdullah

PTI reports:

In New Delhi, the Jammu and Kashmir Chief Minister, Dr. Farooq Abdullah, said today the 72-hour ultimatum given by Gen. Musharraf to Taliban for handing over Osama bin Laden, could be aimed at buying time to clear Pakistani territory of militant camps.

``I wonder why a three-day notice has been given to the Taliban when it has always been heeding diktats from those at the helm of affairs in Islamabad,'' he said before leaving for Srinagar.
