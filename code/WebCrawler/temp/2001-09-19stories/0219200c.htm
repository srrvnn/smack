India for global efforts to tackle terrorism

By Our Special Correspondent

NEW DELHI, SEPT. 18. India today reiterated that terrorism across the globe must be rooted out by combining diplomatic, economic and military measures by a broad-based coalition of nations.

``We have to look at what has given rise to the formation of this terrorist international,'' the spokesperson of the Ministry of External Affairs (MEA) said.

She made these observations when asked whether the fight against terrorism should only target the Saudi fugitive, Osama bin Laden. Government sources said that India wanted that the fight against terrorism should be global in sweep and should not exclude the extremist networks in Pakistan-occupied Kashmir (PoK) and the Northern Areas.

Notwithstanding the stepped up interaction between Pakistan and the U.S. in the wake of last Tuesday's terrorist attacks, the official view is that India would continue to sustain its multifaceted interaction with Washington.

India, while offering full support to the U.S. in the international counter-terrorism campaign, is, however, likely to frankly state its specific national security concerns which it expects to be addressed.

The upcoming meetings between the National Security Adviser, Mr. Brajesh Mishra, and his U.S. counterpart, Ms. Condoleeza Rice, and that of the External Affairs Minister Mr. Jaswant Singh in the U.S. State Department and the Department of Defence are of ``crucial importance'' in crystallising a common response to the challenge posed by international terrorism. Mr. Mishra, who has concluded his visit to Moscow is now on his way to Washington.

Shared anxiety

Vladimir Radyuhin reports from Moscow:

Both India and Russia are anxious to determine how far the U.S. is prepared to go in its military action against terrorist bases. ``We do not know yet what kind of action the U.S. is going to take,'' Mr. Brajesh Mishra told newsmen here before leaving for Washington. ``If it is pin-pointed strikes, the consequences may not be that bad.''

The negative fall-out for the region can also be reduced if the struggle against the Taliban is carried on by Afghans themselves.

``If we are talking about general international effort against the Taliban, then what we would be looking for is support for the Northern Alliance to act against the Taliban,'' Mr. Mishra said.
