Hand over Osama, U.N. tells Taliban

UNITED NATIONS, SEPT. 19. The United Nations Security Council said on Tuesday it had one message for Afghanistan's Taliban rulers: hand over the suspected terrorist, Osama bin Laden, and close all terrorist training camps ``immediately and unconditionally''.

It issued a short statement after a briefing by the Undersecretary-General for Political Affairs, Mr. Kieran Prendergast, on the political, military and humanitarian situation in Afghanistan, ``including the dire consequences of Taliban rule for the Afghan people''.

``There is one and only one message the Security Council has for the Taliban: implement United Nations Security Council resolutions ... immediately and unconditionally,'' said the statement, read by France's U.N. Ambassador, Mr. Jean-David Levitte, the current council president.

It singled out a resolution adopted last December which demanded that the Taliban turn over Osama to the U.S. for trial in the U.S. Embassy bombings in Kenya and Tanzania in August 1998 - or to a third country ``where he will be arrested and effectively brought to justice.''

The resolution also demanded that the Taliban stop providing sanctuary and training for international terrorists, take measures to ensure that its territory was not used for terrorist acts against other states or their citizens and act swiftly to close all terrorist training camps.

The Security Council froze Taliban assets and imposed an international flight ban on Afghanistan's Ariana airlines in November 1999 to pressure the Taliban into handing over Osama. It added an arms embargo on the Taliban in January.

Earlier on Tuesday, Afghanistan's U.N. Ambassador, Mr. Ravan Farhadi, who represents the ousted government of Mr. Burhanuddin Rabbani, offered 15,000 fighters for any operation against Osama or the Taliban.

The U.S. has not asked for assistance from the anti-Taliban forces, which control about 5 per cent of the country in the north. ``We have 15,000 people ready to fight. They are trained to fight the Taliban,'' Mr. Farhadi told a news conference.

He alleged that Pakistan's military intelligence knew where Osama was hiding since it had trained and worked with the Taliban. Mr. Farhadi called for a Security Council meeting to discuss Afghanistan.

Session put off

The United Nations has postponed the annual session of its General Assembly scheduled to be held on September 24 following the terrorist attacks in the U.S. Regional groups decided this after discussions over the last two days.

The General Assembly spokeswoman, Ms. Jan Fischer, said the session had been put off and a new date would be fixed in consultation with the U.S.

- AP, PTI
