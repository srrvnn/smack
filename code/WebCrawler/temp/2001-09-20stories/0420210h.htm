General alert sounded in State

By Our Special Correspondent

HUBLI, SEPT. 19. A general alert has been sounded in the State in the wake of terrorist attacks in the U.S. The Additional Director-General of Police (Law and Order), Mr. M. D. Singh, told presspersons here on Wednesday that police all over the State had been asked to be vigilant for any developments which could affect security.

He said there had been no report of any support for Osama bin Laden in the State. Sale of Osama's posters was reported from Kanpur.

Replying to a question, Mr. Singh said incidents in Hubli had no impact on the law and order situation elsewhere in the State. There was tension in Belgaum and Gadag for some time because two of the victims were from those districts. The situation had eased, he added.

Mr. Singh clarified that there was no question of permitting the Bajrang Dal to organise a demonstration in Hubli on Friday as part of its State-wide protest against the incidents in that city, as prohibitory orders were in force.

Night curfew extended: Hubli was free from untoward incidents on Tuesday and Wednesday, he said. While it had been decided to extend night curfew till Saturday, the question of extending day curfew would be decided on a day-to-day basis.

On Thursday, day curfew would be imposed in those areas which were under the purview of six police stations. However, the period of relaxation of curfew in the morning had been increased from three to four hours. Schools and colleges would remain closed till Saturday. Mr. Singh estimated the damage caused by the disturbances in Hubli at Rs. 26 lakhs. (Mr. Kapil Mohan, Deputy Commissioner, said the losses to business was higher).

He said he would consider arranging for transport for people arriving from other places by train. A proposal to limit curfew to select areas within the limits of police stations was being examined, he added.

He said a body with a stomach wound was found near Dollar Colony in the Gokul area on Wednesday. It was identified as that of Rajasaheb Kusugal of Anandnagar. He was not a victim of any communal incident. Mr. Singh said 106 persons had been taken into custody on Tuesday for violating prohibitory orders.
