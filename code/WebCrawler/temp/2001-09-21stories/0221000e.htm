'U.S. should not take any hasty action'

NEW DELHI, SEPT. 20. Prominent Muslim intellectuals today told the U.S. Ambassador, Mr. Robert Blackwill, that the U.S. should not take any ``hasty action'' against Afghanistan without concrete evidence about the involvement of Osama bin Laden in the terrorist attacks.

``We expressed our condemnation to the terrorist attacks and urged the U.S. not to act in haste till concrete proof was available against Osama'', Fuzail Qasimi, president of the Markazi Jamiat-ul-Ulema-i-Hind, said.

Mr. Qasimi, who was part of the 15-member delegation which met Mr. Blackwill, said the ambassador was told that an individual should not be the target. But action should be initiated to get rid of terrorism. They also appreciated the U.S. President, Mr. George W. Bush, visiting the Islamic centre to meet Muslims to allay their apprehensions.

- PTI
