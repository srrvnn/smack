Giving it a bad name

Sir, - The merchants of terror led by the likes of Osama bin Laden (Kesava Menon, Sept. 16) are to be feared not so much for their devilry of crashing hijacked passenger planes into high- rise buildings as for their capacity to ruin religion and human psyche. The jehadic interpretation of the terror acts has so debased the human mind in some countries such as Pakistan, Palestine and Afghanistan that these merchants of terror are eulogised for their `bravery'.

Here lies the biggest danger to mankind in general and to Islam in particular. Physical damage and death are horrendous, but the invasion of mind is a terrible hidden cancer. The line between jehad and terrorism has been so obliterated that people now identify terrorism with Islam. A terrible fact for Islam to bear with.

T.S. Rao,

Vizag, A.P.
