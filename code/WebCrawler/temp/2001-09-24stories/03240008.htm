Foreign scribes banned from visiting refugees

ISLAMABAD, SEPT. 23. Foreign journalists have been banned from visiting Afghan refugee camps near Peshawar by the provincial Government of Pakistan's north-western frontier province as part of security measures amid fears of attacks against them.

Considerable hatred prevails against foreign journalists in the area as people there think that it is due to the ``poisonous propaganda'' by them that the world has started pointing accusing fingers at the Saudi dissident, Osama bin Laden, for terrorist attacks in the U.S., NNI news agency reported today.

Foreign journalists, including BBC and CNN teams, have been forbidden from entering refugee camps of Jalozai, Shamshtoo and Nasir Bagh to interview the inmates as the refugees have been trying to attack the journalists and snatch their cameras, the report said. Over 300 foreign journalists, mostly from western countries, have descended on Pakistan ahead of possible U.S. strikes against Afghanistan.

- PTI
