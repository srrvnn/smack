Double standard

Sir, - The barbaric attacks in the U.S. were no doubt a brutal deed against humanity. But who are the Taliban? Who is this Osama bin Laden? The Taliban were trained by the U.S. and Pakistan to fight the Russian troops and Osama was CIA's man. The U.S. Government is harvesting what it cultivated.

The man behind the Mumbai blasts was Dawood Ibrahim, who is alleged to be residing in Pakistan. If the U.S. can think of attacking Afghanistan for giving shelter to Osama, why can't India attack Pakistan for sheltering Dawood Ibrahim? Will the U.N., NATO and other countries at least give moral support to India?

R. Vijay Srinivasan,

Coimbatore

* * *

Sir, - The U.S. maintains that the terrorist attacks were a blow to humanity. Were they not aware of this before? India has been facing terrorist attacks for decades. Also, the U.S. is seeking the support of a nation which has been supporting terrorist activities for too long. Most people in Afghanistan don't get food to eat, but are getting funds for arms and resources to develop training camps for terrorists. Americans must be aware of this.

B. Ranganath Rao,

Visakhapatnam

* * *

Sir, - Apropos `Keep off, Musharraf tells India' (Sept. 20), the General's speech highlighted well the conflict between securing the safety of Pakistan and serving the brotherhood of Islam.

Prafull Goradia,

New Delhi
